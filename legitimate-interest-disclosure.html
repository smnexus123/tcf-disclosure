Introduction

The Nexus Engine processes certain personal data based on legitimate interest as a legal basis under Article 6(1)(f) of the GDPR. This page explains our legitimate interests, why processing is necessary, and how we balance our interests against your rights and freedoms.

As required by the IAB Europe Transparency & Consent Framework, we have conducted comprehensive Legitimate Interest Assessments (LIAs) for each purpose where we rely on this legal basis.

Your Rights

Right to Object: You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interest at any time. You can exercise this right through the Consent Management Platform (CMP) on publisher websites where our technology operates.

When you object, we will stop processing your personal data for the purposes to which you objected, unless we have compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests, rights, and freedoms.

Legitimate Interest Purposes

Purpose 2: Select Basic Ads
Our Legitimate Interest:
Delivering relevant, non-personalized advertising to support free online content and services. This allows publishers to monetize their content without requiring subscriptions or paywalls.
Why Processing is Necessary:
  • Enables publishers to offer content to users for free
  • Supports the open internet ecosystem
  • Allows advertisers to reach audiences efficiently
  • Cannot be achieved through less intrusive means while maintaining service viability
Balancing Against Your Rights:
  • We process only pseudonymous identifiers and technical data (no names, emails, or directly identifiable information)
  • Ads are contextual or basic, not based on extensive profiling
  • Data retention limited to 90 days
  • You can object at any time through the CMP
  • Strong technical safeguards (encryption, access controls)
Balancing Test Conclusion:
Given the limited nature of data processing, short retention periods, and the societal benefit of supporting free content, we have determined that your interests and fundamental rights are not overridden by our legitimate interests.
Purpose 7: Measure Ad Performance
Our Legitimate Interest:
Measuring advertising effectiveness, preventing fraud, optimizing ad delivery, and ensuring advertisers receive accurate reporting on their campaigns.
Why Processing is Necessary:
  • Prevents advertising fraud and invalid traffic
  • Ensures advertiser budgets are spent effectively
  • Enables optimization of ad placement for better user experience
  • Provides accountability in the advertising ecosystem
  • Essential for maintaining trust in digital advertising
Balancing Against Your Rights:
  • Measurement uses aggregated, pseudonymous data
  • No individual-level profiling or targeting based on measurement data
  • Data retained only as long as necessary (maximum 90 days for raw data)
  • Reports are aggregate and do not identify individuals
  • Prevents fraud that could harm users and publishers
Balancing Test Conclusion:
Measurement is essential for a functioning advertising ecosystem and fraud prevention. The processing is minimal, uses pseudonymous data, and provides significant benefits to all parties including users (better ad experiences, fraud protection). Your rights are not overridden.
Purpose 8: Measure Content Performance
Our Legitimate Interest:
Understanding how content and ads are delivered and viewed to optimize publisher monetization and improve service quality.
Why Processing is Necessary:
  • Helps publishers understand content effectiveness
  • Enables optimization of content delivery and ad placement
  • Improves user experience by identifying technical issues
  • Supports publisher revenue optimization
Balancing Against Your Rights:
  • Processing limited to technical delivery metrics
  • Aggregate reporting only
  • No individual tracking across sites for content preferences
  • Data anonymized after short retention period

Content measurement is necessary for service quality and uses minimal, technical data. The benefits (improved service, better user experience) outweigh the limited privacy impact, and your rights are not overridden.

Generating aggregate insights about audiences to help publishers and advertisers understand market trends and improve their services.

  • Enables publishers to understand their audiences

Market research uses aggregate data and produces anonymized insights that benefit the entire ecosystem. No individual-level impacts occur, and your rights are not overridden.
Purpose 10: Develop and Improve Services
Our Legitimate Interest:
Improving our technology platform, fixing bugs, enhancing performance, and developing new features to provide better service to publishers and users.
Why Processing is Necessary:
  • Identifies and fixes technical issues and bugs
  • Improves page load speeds and user experience
  • Develops new features requested by publishers
  • Enhances security and fraud prevention
  • Cannot effectively improve services without analyzing usage data
Balancing Against Your Rights:
  • Data used for service improvement is pseudonymized
  • Analysis focuses on technical performance, not individual behavior
  • Development data is typically aggregated
  • Improvements benefit all users (faster, more secure service)

Balancing Test Conclusion:
Service improvement processing benefits all users through better performance, security, and functionality. The processing is minimal and technical in nature, and your rights are not overridden.

Special Purposes (No Legal Basis Required)

We also process data for Special Purposes under the TCF framework. These do not require a legal basis as they are essential for the requested service:

Special Purpose 1: Ensure Security, Prevent and Detect Fraud, and Fix Errors
  • Detecting and preventing malicious, fraudulent, or illegal activity
  • Ensuring systems and processes work properly and securely
  • Identifying and repairing errors that impair intended functionality

Why this is a Special Purpose: Security and fraud prevention are essential for protecting users, publishers, and advertisers. This processing is necessary to deliver the service safely and reliably.

Special Purpose 2: Deliver and Present Advertising and Content
  • Providing advertising or content to you
  • Delivering ads and content technically
  • Controlling frequency of ad delivery
  • Ensuring ads are viewable

Why this is a Special Purpose: Technical delivery of ads and content is the core service users and publishers request. This processing is essential to fulfill that request.

Data We Process

When relying on legitimate interest, we process:

  • Technical identifiers: Cookie IDs, device advertising IDs (pseudonymous)
  • Technical data: IP address (country-level only), user agent, browser type
  • Interaction data: Ad views, clicks, page views (timestamp, URL)
  • Performance data: Load times, errors, bid responses

We do NOT collect:

  • Names, email addresses, postal addresses, or phone numbers
  • Special categories of data (health, political opinions, religion, etc.)
  • Precise geolocation data
  • Any directly identifiable personal information

Safeguards We Implement

Technical Safeguards:

  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest
  • Pseudonymization of identifiers
  • Secure server infrastructure in EU data centers
  • Access controls and authentication
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing

Organizational Safeguards:

  • Staff training on data protection and privacy
  • Data protection policies and procedures
  • Limited data retention periods (90 days maximum)
  • Regular compliance audits
  • Designated Data Protection Officer

Transparency Safeguards:

  • Clear information provided through CMPs
  • Public documentation of processing activities
  • Easy-to-use objection mechanisms
  • Accessible privacy policy and contact information

How to Exercise Your Right to Object

Through Consent Management Platforms (CMPs):

  1. Look for the privacy/cookie settings link on publisher websites (usually in the footer or as a popup)
  2. Find "The Nexus Engine" in the vendor list
  3. Toggle off any purposes processed under "Legitimate Interest"
  4. Save your preferences

Your objection will be respected immediately on your next interaction with our service.
